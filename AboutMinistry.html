About us

Located in the heart of the Middle East and the Arab world, The Kingdom of Jordan is the crossroads that brings the Middle East together in most European cities, and the land of Jordan has been the centre of civilizations for 8,000 years, Arabic is the official language, and English is the second language spoken throughout Country.

Higher education began in Jordan at the beginning of the second half of the 20th century, specifically during the 1960s, when a number of teachers' colleges were established in the country, and this institution provided the educational workforce needed to meet the high demand in the educational schools that characterize that era.

The first Jordanian public university, the University of Jordan, was established in 1962, followed by Yarmouk University in 1976, as well as six other public universities established in different parts of the Kingdom since then. In 1989, the Council of Higher Education approved the first policy document authorizing the construction of private universities, the University of Amman was the first private Jordanian university, established in 1990, and since then a dozen other private universities have been established. As for non-university education, it was introduced in community colleges that began in 1981 through the transformation and expansion of existing teachers' colleges, and these institutions are supposed to provide specialized job training, prepare their students to work in middle-level professions, and all community colleges are run and affiliated to the University of Balqa Applied, a Jordanian public university.
